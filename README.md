# Privacy Policy for Oopsie

Last updated: March 30, 2026

## Overview

Oopsie is a browser extension that redacts sensitive information before you send messages to AI assistants. Your privacy is fundamental to what this extension does, and this policy reflects that.

## Data Collection

Oopsie does **not** collect, transmit, or store any personal data on external servers. The extension operates entirely within your browser.

### What we DO store (locally on your device only)

- **Replacement rules:** The find-and-replace rules you create or import, stored in Chrome's local storage (`chrome.storage.local`).
- **Settings:** Your preference for enabling or disabling the preview modal.

### What we DO NOT collect

- We do not collect your name, email address, or any personal identifiers.
- We do not collect or log the text you type into AI assistants.
- We do not collect browsing history or usage analytics.
- We do not transmit any data to external servers, APIs, or third parties.
- We do not use cookies or tracking technologies.

## How the Extension Works

1. The extension runs locally in your browser on supported AI assistant websites (ChatGPT, Claude, Perplexity, Gemini).
2. When you send a message, it checks your text against your configured replacement rules.
3. Matching text is replaced before the message is sent.
4. All processing happens entirely on your device. No data ever leaves your browser.

## Permissions Used

- **storage:** Used to save your replacement rules and settings locally in Chrome. No other permissions are requested.

## Data Sharing

We do not share any data with third parties because we do not collect any data.

## Data Retention

All data is stored locally in your browser. You can delete it at any time by:
- Removing individual rules through the extension popup.
- Uninstalling the extension (which removes all stored data).

## Children's Privacy

Oopsie does not knowingly collect any information from anyone, including children under 13.

## Changes to This Policy

If this policy is updated, the "Last updated" date at the top will be revised. Continued use of the extension after changes constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.
